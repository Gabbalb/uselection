The office of the US PRESIDENT

The Office of the U.S. President is the executive branch of the federal government, headed by the President, who serves as both the head of state and the head of government. The President oversees the execution and enforcement of federal laws, commands the armed forces, engages in diplomacy, appoints federal officials, and has veto power over legislation. This office symbolizes the leadership and policies of the United States domestically and internationally.
